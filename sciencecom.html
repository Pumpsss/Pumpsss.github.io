Science Communications Module


NIExSST



About

The Science Communications project is a 5 weeks project in collaboration with National Institute of Education (NIE) whereby we are tasked to condense a complex research paper into a more comprehensive Pop Science Article for the general public to read.


Final Outcomes

  • Pop Science Article
  • Presentation slides

Skills Acquired

  • Breaking down of complex scientific papers using the 4 Reader Roles
  • Critical thinking skills to transform complicated terminologies to more layman terms while ensuring all facts are accurate and reliable
  • Presentation skills when presenting in front of mass audience at the NIExSST Symposium

My Learning Process


    Week 1:

    Choosing of research paper


  • Topic Chosen:
    • Electrical and structural changes in the heart (specifically the atria) in cardiac disease/disorders
    • by Dr Melissa Neo
  • What is it about?
    • Investigating the enlargement, thickness, action potential duration and conduction velocity of the heart during Hypercardiomyopathy and Atrial Fibrillation

    • Week 2:

    Face-to-face meeting with research mentors

  • Interaction with respective research mentors
    • Able to bring up burning questions regarding complex scientific terminologies to the mentors
      • to understand and clear any misconception regarding their research
  • Tour of lab facilities at NIE
    • Have a better understanding about the equipments stated in the Methodology of the research paper

    Week 3-4:

    Crafting our pop science article


  • Afterwhich, we had subsequent meetings with our research mentors via Zoom
    • Dr Neo kindly taught us basic biological concepts for us to better understand how the heart functions for example,
      • how cardiac diseases such as Hypercardiomyopathy can result in Atrial Fibrillation
      • Cross-bridge calcium cycling and the basic mechanism of Cardiac Action Potential
    • Most importantly, Dr Neo helped us to break down the complex data
      • shown in conduction heterogeneity graphs and immunostaining marker graph of the heart below

Week 5:

Improvement of pop science article


  • Amendments of given feedbacks from research mentors
  • Submission of article

    • Week 6:

    Presentation at NIExSST Symposium @NIE


  • We all were given the opportunity to present in smaller breakout rooms consisting of 4 other groups
